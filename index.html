Sadhguru's Food Wisdom

Pranic Classification

Positive Pranic: All vegetables, fruits, nuts, sprouts, dry fruits — add life energy to your system.

Zero Pranic: Potatoes and tomatoes — neither add nor take away prana; increase sleep quota. Avoid if you have joint problems.

Negative Pranic: Garlic, onion, asafoetida, chilli, brinjal, coffee, alcohol — take away prana and cause emotional instability.

Ekadashi Fasting

On the 11th day after full moon and new moon, the body naturally needs less food. Fasting on Ekadashi lowers cancerous cells — they need 20× more food than normal cells and die first during a fast.

Break the fast with papaya, fruit salads, and green gram sprouts.

Water Wisdom

Store water in a copper vessel overnight — it acquires qualities beneficial for the liver and overall health. Drink tepid water on an empty stomach in the morning as a great cleanser.

Do not drink water immediately after eating — it dilutes digestive juices.

Honey & Jaggery

Honey: Never heat honey — it turns toxic. Add only after removing from heat. Anti-bacterial, anti-microbial and mirrors the composition of human blood.

Jaggery: The medicinal sugar — cleanses toxins, stimulates digestion, helps recovery from heat and exertion. Always preferred over white sugar.

Kitchen Techniques

🥗 Salad Dressings

Classic Lemon & Olive Oil: Whisk lemon juice + salt + pepper, stream in olive oil. Best for green leaf salads, tomatoes, cucumber, capsicum.

Sweet Salad Dressing: Orange juice + lemon + honey + olive oil. Perfect for fruit salads, carrots, beets, spinach.

Asian Vinaigrette: Gingelly oil + sesame seeds + orange + lemon + honey + ginger + soy sauce. Great for slaw-style shredded salads.

🌱 Sprouting Guide

Soak moong dal 6–8 hours until skins split. Wrap in damp cloth, place in warm dark spot for 8 hours. Sprout tails of ¼–½ inch are ideal — too short or too long causes excess gas.

Sprouting maximizes nutrition and reduces cooking time. Most sprouts can be eaten raw; chanas should be blanched first.

🥜 Soaking Groundnuts

Shell raw groundnuts, wash 2–3 times. Cover with warm water (double depth). Add a pinch of salt to reduce enzyme inhibitors. Soak 6–8 hours until plump. Drain, rinse and serve.

Soaking maximizes phytonutrients and digestibility. Serve as a raw side dish at breakfast alongside kanji.

🌶️ Tempering (Tarka)

Heat oil until very hot (not smoking). Add mustard seeds → cumin → other whole spices → dals → curry leaves → aromatics → base vegetables → powdered spices.

This technique infuses oil with deep flavour and mellows pungency of spices.

🍯 Jaggery Syrup

Equal parts jaggery + water in heavy pan. Boil on low heat, skim impurities.

Single-string = for teas, juices, kanjis. Double-string = for pancakes, honey substitute. Paste = for Indian sweets.

🥦 Blanching Vegetables

Cut vegetables into bite-sized pieces. Boil salted water. Add vegetables until colour intensifies (bright green). Immediately plunge into ice-cold water to stop cooking. Drain and use in salads.

Preserves nutrients, colour and texture while making dense vegetables easier to digest.
